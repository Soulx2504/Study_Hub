Flashcards

Acceleration

Vector

A quantity which has both a magnitude and direction

Scalar

A quantity which only has magnitude

Distance

The overall ground covered by an object during its motion

Displacement

The separation between two points in a named direction

Speed

The distance travelled per unit time

Velocity

The rate of change of displacement

Acceleration

The rate of change of velocity

Momentum

The product of a body’s mass and velocity

The Principle Of Conservation Of Momentum

In a closed system where no external forces act, momentum before is equal to momentum after

Force

That which changes the momentum of an object

Newton (Unit)

A force of 1 newton gives an acceleration of 1ms⁻² to a mass of 1kg

Weight

The force at which a mass is attracted to the Earth

Friction

The force that opposes motion between surfaces in contact

Newton’s First Law

A body remains at rest or in constant motion unless acted on by a resultant force

Newton’s Second Law

The rate of change of momentum is directly proportional to the applied force and takes place in the same direction as the applied force

Newton’s Third Law

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction

Circular Motion

Angular Velocity

The angle (measured in radians) swept out per second.

Centripetal Acceleration

The acceleration that a body which is moving in a circle has towards the centre of the circle.

Centripetal Force

The force acting towards the centre that is needed to keep a body moving in a circle.

Periodic Time

The time taken to complete one full revolution.

Pressure, Gravity and Moments

Torque

Torque is the moment of force. The product of the force applied and the perpendicular distance to the fulcrum.

Lever

A rigid body which is free to rotate about a fixed point.

A Couple

Two parallel and equal forces that act in opposite directions.

Conditions For Static Equilibrium

Firstly, the net force acting upon the object must be zero. Secondly, the net torque acting upon the object must also be zero.

Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation

The force felt between two bodies is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Centre of Gravity

The point of an object through which the weight of that object appears to act.

Kepler’s Third Law

The square of the period of a satellite is directly proportional to the cube of its radius of orbit and inversely proportional to the mass of the planet it is orbiting.

Density

Mass per unit volume.

Pressure

Force per unit area.

Archimedes’ Principle

When a body is fully or partially immersed in a fluid, it experiences an upthrust which is equivalent to the weight of the fluid displaced.

Law of Flotation

A floating body will displace its own weight of fluid.

Boyle’s Law

For a fixed mass of gas at a constant temperature, volume is inversely proportional to pressure.

Simple Harmonic Motion

Hooke’s Law

When an object is bent, stretched or compressed by a displacement s, the restoring force F is directly proportional to the displacement, provided the elastic limit is not exceeded.

Simple Harmonic Motion

Motion where the object’s acceleration is directly proportional to its displacement from a mean position and is always directed towards this mean position.
